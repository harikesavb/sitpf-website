A Movement for Temple Justice

The South India Temples' Protection Foundation was established on 1st April 2026 out of a deep and abiding commitment to protect Hindu temples, their sacred properties and endowments from encroachment, mismanagement and unlawful interference across India.

Constituted as an irrevocable, non-profit and non-political public charitable and religious trust under the Indian Trusts Act, the Foundation operates within the framework of the Constitution of India and all applicable laws, ensuring its benefits extend to the general public without discrimination of caste, creed, sect, gender or community.

Founder President Thiru K.S. Hariharan, driven by lifelong dedication to Sanatana Dharma, has assembled a distinguished Board of Trustees committed to transparent and accountable temple administration and the preservation of India's irreplaceable spiritual and cultural heritage.

The Foundation's activities remain charitable in nature within the meaning of Section 2(15) of the Income Tax Act, 1961, and registrations under Sections 12A and 80G are applied for, enabling tax-exempt donations from individuals and institutions.

Our Purpose

Vision & Mission

Our Vision
Guardians of Sacred Heritage

To ensure the protection, preservation, restoration and lawful safeguarding of Hindu temples, temple properties, sacred endowments and spiritual traditions across India — thereby upholding the constitutional rights of devotees and preserving the civilization and cultural legacy of Sanatana Dharma for present and future generations.

Our Mission
Action for Temple Justice

To identify, document and legally safeguard temple lands and endowments; to initiate civil actions, writ petitions and public interest litigations; to assist in the restoration and conservation of heritage temples; to support temple priests, traditional artisans and workers; and to promote transparent and accountable temple administration within the bounds of constitutional governance.

What We Do

Our Four Core Objectives

The Foundation's activities remain charitable in nature within the meaning of Section 2(15) of the Income Tax Act, 1961 — serving the public good without discrimination of religion, caste, gender or community.

Temple Protection
  • Safeguarding temple properties and endowments from encroachment
  • Preventing illegal occupation or alienation of temple assets
  • Assisting in recovery of misappropriated temple funds and lands
  • Documenting and preserving official temple land records
  • Strengthening security and administrative oversight of temples
Legal Intervention
  • Appointing advocates and legal teams for temple causes
  • Filing writ petitions under Articles 32 & 226 of the Constitution
  • Challenging unlawful administrative actions affecting temples
  • Supporting Public Interest Litigations for temple rights
  • Engaging in civil disputes for recovery of temple properties
Documentation & Research
  • Digitization and preservation of historic temple records and inscriptions
  • Conducting historical and legal research on temple endowments
  • Publishing awareness materials on temple protection rights
  • Maintaining a national database of temple encroachments
  • Collaborating with historians, archaeologists and heritage experts
Conservation & Cultural Support
  • Assisting in renovation of heritage and dilapidated temples
  • Supporting conservation of sculptures, inscriptions and murals
  • Promoting traditional temple arts, classical music and rituals
  • Supporting temple priests (archakas) and traditional artisans
  • Raising public awareness about the value of sacred heritage
How We Operate

Programs & Activities

The Foundation undertakes a diverse range of programs rooted in law, heritage, community and advocacy to protect temples across India.

⚖️
Legal Aid Cell

Maintaining a dedicated legal team that identifies and addresses encroachments, files writ petitions, and represents temple interests in courts across India. We provide pro-bono legal support for deserving temple causes.

🗺️
Land Survey & Documentation

Conducting field surveys of temple properties, digitizing ancient records and deeds, and creating a comprehensive geo-tagged database of temple lands and boundaries to prevent future encroachments.

🏛️
Heritage Temple Restoration

Identifying neglected and dilapidated temples across South India and facilitating their restoration with support from donors, state governments and heritage organizations, ensuring traditional Agama Shastra compliance.

📚
Awareness & Education

Publishing booklets, conducting seminars and workshops to educate devotees, priests and the public on their constitutional rights regarding temple governance and the legal framework protecting Hindu endowments.

🪔
Priest & Artisan Support

Advocating for fair remuneration and welfare of hereditary temple priests (archakas), traditional sculptors (sthapathis), musicians and artisans — the custodians of living temple traditions.

📣
Public Interest Advocacy

Engaging with media, legislators and civil society to raise awareness about temple governance reform, the inequitable application of religious endowment laws, and constitutional rights of Hindu communities.

Structure & Compliance

Governance Framework

The Foundation is governed by a robust, transparent framework enshrined in the Trust Deed, ensuring accountability to its beneficiaries and compliance with all applicable Indian laws.

3–9
Board Strength
Minimum 3, maximum 9 trustees ensuring focused yet diverse governance with continuity clauses
Q
Quarterly Meetings
Board meets at least once per quarter with 7-day advance notice to all trustees
85%
Income to Objects
Minimum 85% of all income applied exclusively toward the Trust's charitable objectives
3yr
Trustee Term
Office bearers serve 3-year terms and are eligible for reappointment by Board resolution
Decision-Making Thresholds
⚖️
Simple Majority
Ordinary resolutions of the Board are passed by simple majority of trustees present and voting at duly constituted meetings with quorum.
⅔
Two-Thirds Majority
Removal of a trustee from office requires a two-thirds majority vote of the full Board of Trustees, following due process.
¾
Three-Fourths Majority
Amendments to the Trust Deed require a three-fourths majority. No amendment may alter the charitable character, irrevocable nature, or the core temple protection objectives.
✓
Unanimous Resolution
Dissolution of the Trust requires a unanimous resolution. Upon dissolution, remaining assets shall be transferred exclusively to a similar registered charitable trust serving public benefit.
Financial Compliance & Controls
🏛️
Tax Registrations
The Trust shall apply for and maintain registrations under Sections 12A and 80G of the Income Tax Act, 1961 to enable tax-exempt donations from individuals and corporates.
📋
Annual Audit
Accounts are audited annually by a qualified Chartered Accountant. The audited financial statement is placed before the Board for approval and maintained for public inspection.
🏦
Bank Account Operations
All banking instruments are jointly operated by any two Treasurers, with prior written approval from the Founder President and Vice-President for significant transactions.
🌍
Foreign Contributions
Foreign contributions shall be received only after obtaining FCRA 2010 registration and shall be maintained in a designated bank account, utilized strictly in accordance with applicable law.
Legal Notice

Official Declaration

This Trust is registered as a Public Charitable and Religious Trust under the Indian Trusts Act. All donations are intended to be eligible for tax exemption under Section 80G of the Income Tax Act, 1961 (registration applied for). The Trust operates strictly on a non-profit, non-political and irrevocable basis. Income and properties of the Trust are applied solely towards the promotion of its stated charitable and religious objects. No portion thereof shall be distributed, directly or indirectly, as profit or benefit to any trustee or private individual, in accordance with the provisions of the Income Tax Act, 1961 and other applicable laws of India.
